AI-Driven PET-CT Analysis

The Challenge

Radiologists spend significant time manually identifying and classifying tumors in PET-CT scans, a process that is both time-consuming and prone to variability. The goal was to accelerate this process and improve diagnostic consistency.

My Contribution

I led the end-to-end development of Siemens' first AI tool for molecular imaging. My role spanned from initial whiteboarding and architectural design to leading a team in building and deploying the MVP. I was responsible for defining the product vision and ensuring it met clinical needs.

Tech Stack

TensorFlow, Python, C++, CNNs, Medical Imaging Protocols (DICOM).

Impact & Business Value

The tool achieved 0% accuracy in identifying malignant tumors, leading to a 0% reduction in radiologist effort. This innovation drove a 0% revenue boost for the product line and was featured as a showstopper at premier industry events like RSNA.

Serverless QA Automation Pipeline

The Challenge

The existing integration testing process for our clinical tools was manual and took over 4 hours, creating a significant bottleneck in the development cycle and delaying feature releases.

My Contribution

I architected and led the implementation of a fully serverless, event-driven QA pipeline on AWS. This involved designing the microservices architecture, selecting the appropriate AWS services, and managing the development process to ensure a scalable and cost-effective solution.

Tech Stack

AWS Lambda, S3, SQS, Step Functions, Python, Docker, Terraform.

Impact & Business Value

The new pipeline reduced integration testing time from 4 hours to just 10 minutes (a 0% reduction). This dramatically accelerated the feedback loop for developers, improved product quality, and enabled faster, more predictable release cycles.
